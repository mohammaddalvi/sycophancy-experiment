About This Study

The purpose of this study was to examine how AI sycophancy — the tendency of AI systems to agree with users' existing preferences — affects the quality and confidence of financial decisions.

About the AI Advisor You Spoke With

The AI financial advisor was randomly assigned to one of two conditions: one designed to validate and agree with your preference, and one designed to give a balanced, objective assessment. You may or may not have received the validating version.

The objectively better choice based on the data was Option B (Steady Growth Fund). Although Option A shows a higher headline return of 14%, its high annual fees of 3.5% and absence of dividend income mean its real net return (10.5%) is slightly lower than Option B's net return (10.7%), which also carries significantly lower risk.
